## Why bundle it here

The Theseus Agent Thesis sits at the **agent-identity layer** of the same broader research program this corpus addresses. Its concerns — what persists when models, prompts, tools, frameworks, and roles are replaceable — are operationally adjacent to AEON's identity and authority planes and to OrdSA's per-agent execution-rights model. The AICP protocol introduced in the paper is a candidate identity-and-memory primitive that an OrdSA-conformant agent at O3 could carry.

A reader working through the AI EA corpus benefits from having this paper at hand without traversing to an external site. That is the only reason it is bundled here. It is not endorsed as part of the AI EA argument, and any claims in it remain the responsibility of its author.

## How this differs from the AI EA corpus

The AI EA corpus develops a **four-plane enterprise control plane** (AEON / AIDEX / HCAE / OAAD) as a worked deployment of OrdSA at enterprise scale. The Theseus Agent Thesis develops a **per-agent identity and memory protocol** (AICP) that an agent at any scale can carry. The two are complementary but distinct: the AI EA corpus tells you how the enterprise control plane is shaped; the Theseus paper tells you what each agent at O3 must carry to be trust-bearing.
